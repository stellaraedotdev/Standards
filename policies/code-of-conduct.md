# Code of Conduct Policy

## Purpose
Define expected behavior so contributors, maintainers, and community members can collaborate safely and respectfully.

## Scope
Applies to all project spaces, including repositories, issue trackers, pull requests, chats, meetings, events, and public or private community channels.

## Expected Behavior
- Be respectful, professional, and considerate.
- Give constructive feedback and focus on the work, not the person.
- Respect differing viewpoints, experience levels, and backgrounds.
- Share credit appropriately and communicate clearly.

## Unacceptable Behavior
- Harassment, intimidation, discrimination, or abuse.
- Derogatory language, slurs, or personal attacks.
- Retaliation against reporters or witnesses.
- Doxxing, stalking, or unwanted contact.
- Deliberate disruption of collaboration spaces.

## Reporting and Response
- Report concerns to the designated maintainers or conduct contacts.
- Reports should be handled promptly, discreetly, and respectfully.
- Investigations should preserve privacy as much as practical.
- Outcomes should be based on documented facts and policy impact.

## Enforcement Actions
- Informal warning.
- Content removal or edit request.
- Temporary moderation or participation restrictions.
- Permanent removal from project spaces when necessary.
- Escalation to platform or legal authorities when required by law or safety concerns.

## Protection Against Retaliation
- Retaliation against good-faith reports is prohibited.
- Witnesses and reporters should not be penalized for raising concerns.
- Patterned retaliation must be treated as a serious policy violation.

## Rule Severity
- MUST: Standards required for participation.
- SHOULD: Strong expectations with limited exceptions.
- MAY: Helpful practices that support a healthier community.

## Review Checklist
- Behavior expectations are clear and visible.
- Reporting paths are documented.
- Enforcement options are proportional and actionable.
- Non-retaliation protections are explicit.
