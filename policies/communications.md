# Communications Policy

## Purpose
Define how Stellarae projects should communicate so messaging is accurate, respectful, and consistent.

## Scope
Applies to project announcements, support conversations, community spaces, issue trackers, reviews, meetings, and external statements.

## Communication Principles
- Be clear, concise, and factual.
- Match tone to audience and context.
- Avoid speculation presented as fact.
- Use respectful language in all channels.

## Internal Communication
- Keep internal updates relevant and action-oriented.
- Share decisions with enough context to support execution.
- Escalate blockers and risks early.
- Document important decisions in durable locations.

## External Communication
- Ensure public statements are reviewed when needed.
- Do not disclose confidential, personal, or security-sensitive information.
- Coordinate messaging for incidents, releases, and policy changes.
- Provide accurate timelines and avoid overpromising.

## Incident Communication
- Use a designated communication owner for incidents.
- Share what is known, what is unknown, and what is being done.
- Update stakeholders as facts change.
- Close the loop with a post-incident summary when appropriate.

## Community Conduct
- Keep discussions professional and on topic.
- De-escalate disagreement where possible.
- Do not use communications channels to harass or pressure others.
- Respect moderator and maintainer decisions.

## Records and Traceability
- Keep important decisions in a persistent record.
- Preserve approved announcements and policy changes.
- Use searchable channels for technical decisions when practical.

## Rule Severity
- MUST: Required communication behavior for official channels.
- SHOULD: Strong practice for clarity and trust.
- MAY: Optional improvement for coordination or transparency.

## Review Checklist
- Message accuracy and tone were reviewed.
- Sensitive information was excluded.
- Incident or release communications were coordinated.
- Durable records were created for important decisions.
