# Licensing Policy

## Purpose
Define how Stellarae documents, code, and related assets may be used, modified, and distributed.

## Scope
Applies to all repository contents unless a file-level or project-level exception is clearly stated.

## Default License Position
- Repository contents are licensed under the terms stated in the repository root or in the relevant file notice.
- When no alternate notice exists, the repository default governs.
- Contributors must not add third-party content without confirming compatible licensing.

## Contributor Responsibilities
- Submit only work that you have rights to contribute.
- Disclose third-party material, borrowed text, or embedded assets.
- Preserve existing notices and attribution requirements.
- Respect upstream licenses, including notice and source obligations.

## Third-Party Materials
- Track external dependencies, assets, and copied examples.
- Verify compatibility before inclusion.
- Keep attribution and license text with redistributed assets when required.
- Remove materials that cannot be used legally or compliantly.

## Redistribution and Modification
- Keep license notices intact when redistributing covered materials.
- Clearly identify modifications where required by the source license.
- Do not imply ownership of third-party work.
- Follow stricter license terms when multiple licenses apply.

## Compatibility Review
- Review license obligations before adopting dependencies or examples.
- Escalate unclear or conflicting terms before merge.
- Prefer permissive, well-understood licenses for shared standards when practical.

## Exceptions
- Any exception must be documented in writing and approved by maintainers.
- Exceptions should describe scope, duration, and mitigation.
- Temporary exceptions should be removed or renewed explicitly.

## Rule Severity
- MUST: Legal or contractual requirement that cannot be waived casually.
- SHOULD: Strong expectation for compliant reuse and attribution.
- MAY: Optional documentation that improves clarity.

## Review Checklist
- Source rights and compatibility were checked.
- Required notices and attribution were preserved.
- Exceptions, if any, are documented.
- Redistribution obligations are understood.
